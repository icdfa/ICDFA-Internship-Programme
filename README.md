## 1. Programme Overview

The ICDFA Cybersecurity Internship Programme (CIP) is a structured, 6-month, lab-intensive internship designed to provide aspiring cybersecurity professionals with foundational-to-intermediate hands-on skills in either Ethical Hacking or Digital Forensics.

The programme is divided into two distinct specialisation tracks. Interns must select one track upon enrolment and complete it in full before they are eligible to enrol in the second track. Both tracks cannot be undertaken simultaneously.

The programme is delivered through the NDG NETLAB+ platform in partnership with Cisco Networking Academy, Palo Alto Networks, and other institutional partners. All courses include hands-on labs, chapter exams, midterm exams, and final comprehensive exams to ensure mastery of each subject area.

Upon successful completion of a track, interns are awarded the ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI) credential with the relevant specialisation endorsement.

---

## 2. Programme Structure

The programme offers two specialisation tracks, each lasting 26 weeks (6 months):

### Track A: Ethical Hacking (26 Weeks)

10 courses covering Linux systems, network security, ethical hacking, critical infrastructure, security operations, security fundamentals, and cloud security. Total of 148 labs.

### Track B: Digital Forensics (26 Weeks)

7 courses covering Linux systems, network security, security fundamentals, digital forensics, security operations, and forensics case studies. Total of 122 labs.

Both tracks share a common foundation in Linux systems administration and network security before diverging into their respective specialisation areas.

---

## 3. Track Selection Policy

Interns must choose one track at the time of enrolment. The following rules apply:

- Interns cannot enrol in both tracks simultaneously
- Interns must complete their selected track in full, including all labs, exams, and assessments, before they are eligible to apply for the second track
- There is no automatic enrolment into the second track. Interns who wish to pursue the second track must submit a new application after completing the first
- Credits and completed courses from one track that overlap with the other track (e.g., Introduction to Linux 1, Introduction to Linux 2, Network Security Fundamentals) may be recognised, subject to approval by the Director of Training
- Interns who fail or withdraw from a track must wait for the next available intake to re-apply

## 8. Assessment Framework

### Grading Scale

- **Distinction:** 90-100%
- **Merit:** 80-89%
- **Pass:** 70-79%
- **Fail:** Below 70%

### Passing Requirements

A minimum of 70% is required to pass each course. Interns must achieve at least 70% in each individual course to progress to the next course.

### Assessment Components

- **Continuous Assessment (60%):** Lab completion and grading, chapter exams
- **Final Assessment (40%):** Midterm exams, final exams, capstone project evaluation

### No-Retake Policy

Under the no-retake policy, if an intern fails any course or lab, they cannot retake it within the same intake. They must reapply for a future intake to retry the course.

### Grade Appeals

Interns may submit a grade appeal within 5 business days of receiving their grade. The appeal must include a detailed explanation of why they believe the grade is incorrect, supported by evidence.

## 10. Programme Policies

### Attendance

Interns must maintain a minimum attendance rate of 90% across all scheduled sessions, labs, exams, and mandatory events.

### Lab Discipline

Interns must adhere strictly to all lab discipline policies including proper use of credentials, no content sharing, and respect for lab infrastructure.

### Academic Integrity

All work submitted must be original and the intern's own. Plagiarism, collusion, cheating, and other forms of academic dishonesty are strictly prohibited.

### Code of Conduct

Interns must conduct themselves professionally and respectfully at all times, treating instructors, peers, and staff with respect and professionalism.

### Confidentiality

Interns must respect the confidentiality of lab content, proprietary tools, and other ICDFA intellectual property.

## 11. Certification and Credential

### ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI)

Upon successful completion of either Track A or Track B, interns are awarded the ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI) credential with a specialisation endorsement:

- **CCI -- Ethical Hacking Specialisation** (for Track A graduates)
- **CCI -- Digital Forensics Specialisation** (for Track B graduates)

### Industry Certifications

Interns completing the programme are eligible for and receive exam coupons for:

- **LPI LPIC-1 Certification** (Linux Professional Institute)
- **CompTIA Security+ Certification** (CompTIA)

### Credential Validity

The CCI credential is valid indefinitely and demonstrates foundational-to-intermediate competency in cybersecurity.
