## 1. About the ICDFA Charity Foundation

### 1.1 Mission

The ICDFA Charity Foundation exists to democratise cybersecurity education by providing 100% fully funded scholarships to talented individuals who lack financial resources to access world-class training. We believe that **talent is universal, but opportunity is not**.

### 1.2 Vision

Our vision is to build a diverse, globally-connected community of cybersecurity professionals who are equipped to defend critical infrastructure, protect communities, and advance the field of cybersecurity.

### 1.3 History

The ICDFA Charity Foundation was established in 2024 as a non-profit charitable organisation dedicated to supporting underrepresented and underprivileged individuals in cybersecurity. Since its inception, the Foundation has provided scholarships to hundreds of aspiring cybersecurity professionals across multiple continents.

### 1.4 Values

- **Accessibility**: Cybersecurity education should be accessible to all, regardless of financial circumstances
- **Diversity**: We actively seek to build a diverse community of cybersecurity professionals
- **Excellence**: We maintain the highest standards of training and education
- **Community**: We believe in giving back and supporting future generations
- **Integrity**: We operate with transparency and accountability

## 2. Scholarship Overview

### 2.1 What is the ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship?

The ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship is a 100% fully funded scholarship that covers all tuition and fees for the ICDFA Cybersecurity Internship Programme (CIP). The scholarship is designed for talented individuals who demonstrate financial need and a genuine commitment to pursuing a career in cybersecurity.

### 2.2 Scholarship Value

The total scholarship value is **USD $150 per recipient**, which covers:

- 100% of programme tuition fees
- Full access to all lab environments and learning resources
- Access to partner platforms (NDG NETLAB+, Cisco, Palo Alto Networks, Microsoft Azure, AWS)
- Certification exam coupons (LPI LPIC-1 101 and 102)
- ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI) credential upon completion

### 2.3 Scholarship Duration

The scholarship covers the entire 26-week CIP programme for one track (either Track A: Ethical Hacking or Track B: Digital Forensics).

### 2.4 Scholarship Availability

The ICDFA Charity Foundation awards scholarships on a rolling basis, with up to 50 scholarship slots available per CIP intake. Scholarship availability depends on funding from donors and grants.

## 3. Commitment Fee

### 3.1 What is the Commitment Fee?

The commitment fee is a one-time payment of **USD $10** that scholarship recipients must pay upon enrolment. This fee is separate from the tuition fee (which is fully covered by the scholarship).

### 3.2 Purpose of the Commitment Fee

The commitment fee serves two purposes:

1. **Demonstrates Commitment**: The fee demonstrates the scholar's genuine commitment to completing the programme. Research shows that even a small financial investment increases completion rates.

2. **Sustainability**: The fee contributes to the Foundation's operational costs and enables us to award more scholarships to future cohorts.

### 3.3 Is the Commitment Fee Refundable?

No, the commitment fee is non-refundable. However, if you withdraw from the programme before it starts, you may request a refund within 14 days of payment.

### 3.4 How Do I Pay the Commitment Fee?

Payment methods include: credit card (Visa, Mastercard, American Express), bank transfer, and PayPal. Payment instructions will be provided upon scholarship confirmation.

## 4. Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship, you must meet ALL of the following criteria:

### 4.1 Basic Requirements

- Be at least 18 years of age
- Possess a high school diploma, GED, or equivalent qualification
- Have reliable internet access and a computer meeting minimum technical specifications
- Be able to commit 40-50 hours per week for 26 consecutive weeks
- Be a citizen or permanent resident of an eligible country (see list below)

### 4.2 Financial Need

- Demonstrate financial need through submission of financial documents (tax returns, pay stubs, bank statements, etc.)
- Have a household income below the poverty line or a significant financial hardship
- Be unable to afford the USD $150 programme fee without scholarship support

### 4.3 Academic Readiness

- Score "Partially Ready" or above on the pre-internship readiness assessment
- Demonstrate basic computer literacy and command-line familiarity
- Show evidence of commitment to learning and professional development

### 4.4 Eligible Countries

The ICDFA Charity Foundation currently awards scholarships to residents of the following regions:

- Sub-Saharan Africa
- South Asia (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka)
- Southeast Asia (Philippines, Indonesia, Vietnam, Thailand, Cambodia)
- Latin America
- Middle East and North Africa
- Other regions as funding permits

*Note: Eligibility may expand as the Foundation secures additional funding.*

### 4.5 Non-Discrimination

The ICDFA Charity Foundation does not discriminate based on race, ethnicity, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, religion, disability, or any other protected characteristic. We actively encourage applications from underrepresented groups in cybersecurity.

## 5. Application Process

### 5.1 Step 1: Complete the CIP Application Form

Begin by completing the standard CIP application form at www.icdfa.org/cip-apply. Indicate that you are interested in the scholarship during this step.

### 5.2 Step 2: Submit Scholarship-Specific Documents

In addition to the standard CIP application documents, scholarship applicants must submit:

- **Financial Needs Statement** (1-2 pages): Describe your financial situation, why you need the scholarship, and how the scholarship will impact your life and career.
- **Financial Documentation**: Provide recent tax returns, pay stubs, or bank statements to verify financial need.
- **Letter of Recommendation** (optional but encouraged): A letter from a teacher, employer, mentor, or community leader who can speak to your character, commitment, and potential.
- **Essay** (500-750 words): "Why I Want to Pursue a Career in Cybersecurity" - describe your motivation, career goals, and how the CIP will help you achieve them.

### 5.3 Step 3: Complete the Pre-Internship Readiness Assessment

Take the diagnostic quiz to evaluate your readiness. Scoring "Partially Ready" or above is required for scholarship eligibility.

### 5.4 Step 4: Attend the Scholarship Interview

Scholarship applicants participate in an extended 45-minute interview with a Scholarship Committee member. The interview assesses your motivation, financial need, commitment, and suitability for the scholarship programme.

### 5.5 Step 5: Receive Scholarship Decision

Scholarship decisions are communicated within 10 business days of your interview. Approved scholars receive a scholarship award letter with details about the scholarship, commitment fee, and next steps.

### 5.6 Step 6: Confirm Scholarship Acceptance

Upon receiving your award letter, you must confirm your acceptance of the scholarship within 5 business days. This confirms your commitment to the programme and your obligation to complete the CDS requirement.

### 5.7 Step 7: Pay the Commitment Fee and Enrol

Pay the USD $10 commitment fee and complete the enrolment process. Upon completion, you will receive your NDG NETLAB+ account credentials and orientation instructions.

## 6. Selection Criteria

The ICDFA Charity Foundation uses a holistic review process to select scholarship recipients. The following criteria are considered:

### 6.1 Financial Need (40% weight)

- Demonstrated financial hardship and inability to afford the programme fee
- Household income relative to regional poverty line
- Supporting financial documentation

### 6.2 Academic Readiness (25% weight)

- Pre-internship readiness assessment score
- Educational background and academic performance
- Demonstrated commitment to learning

### 6.3 Motivation and Career Goals (20% weight)

- Clarity and realism of career goals
- Demonstrated passion for cybersecurity
- Alignment with the Foundation's mission

### 6.4 Diversity and Inclusion (10% weight)

- Representation from underrepresented groups in cybersecurity
- Potential to contribute to a diverse cybersecurity community
- Commitment to mentoring and supporting others

### 6.5 Community Impact (5% weight)

- Potential to contribute back to the community through CDS
- Commitment to supporting future generations
- Alignment with the Foundation's values

## 8. Scholar Obligations

### 8.1 Academic Performance

Scholarship recipients are expected to maintain strong academic performance. Specific obligations include:

- Achieve a minimum grade of **70%** in each course
- Maintain an overall average of at least **75%** across all courses
- Complete all labs and assessments on time
- Attend all mandatory sessions and events

Failure to meet these academic standards may result in scholarship termination and reclamation of funds.

### 8.2 Attendance

- Maintain a minimum attendance rate of **90%** across all scheduled sessions, labs, exams, and mandatory events
- Notify the Director of Training in advance of any anticipated absences
- Provide documentation for excused absences

Excessive absences may result in scholarship termination.

### 8.3 Conduct and Integrity

- Adhere to all ICDFA policies and procedures
- Maintain academic integrity and honesty in all assessments
- Follow the Code of Conduct and lab discipline policies
- Treat instructors, peers, and staff with respect and professionalism

Violations may result in scholarship termination and programme dismissal.

### 8.4 Community Development Service (CDS)

- Complete a minimum of **40 hours** of Community Development Service during or immediately after the internship
- Engage in approved CDS activities that benefit the community
- Maintain a CDS activity log with supervisor sign-offs
- Submit a reflection report upon completion

CDS is a mandatory obligation for all scholarship recipients (see Section 9 for details).

### 8.5 Confidentiality and IP Protection

- Respect the confidentiality of lab content and proprietary tools
- Not share lab credentials or access with any non-enrolled individual
- Not record, screenshot, or distribute lab content without authorisation
- Protect ICDFA intellectual property

## 9. Community Development Service (CDS)

### 9.1 What is CDS?

Community Development Service (CDS) is a mandatory requirement for all ICDFA Charity Foundation scholarship recipients. CDS is a way for scholars to give back to their communities and support future generations of cybersecurity professionals.

### 9.2 CDS Objectives

CDS aims to:

- Democratise access to cybersecurity knowledge and skills
- Build awareness of cybersecurity threats and best practices in underserved communities
- Support youth and underprivileged individuals in pursuing cybersecurity careers
- Strengthen the cybersecurity community through mentorship and collaboration

### 9.3 CDS Hours Required

Scholarship recipients must complete a minimum of **40 hours** of CDS. This can be completed during the internship or within 3 months after completion.

### 9.4 Approved CDS Activities

Approved CDS activities include:

- **Cybersecurity Awareness Workshops**: Conduct workshops on cybersecurity best practices, password security, phishing awareness, etc. for schools, community organisations, or NGOs
- **IT Mentoring**: Mentor underprivileged youth or students interested in pursuing cybersecurity careers
- **Community Digital Literacy Training**: Teach basic computer skills, internet safety, and digital literacy to community members
- **Open-Source Contributions**: Contribute to open-source cybersecurity projects or tools
- **Technical Support for NGOs**: Provide IT support and cybersecurity advice to local NGOs or non-profits
- **Cybersecurity Content Creation**: Write blog posts, create videos, or develop educational content on cybersecurity topics
- **Peer Tutoring**: Tutor other CIP interns or future cohorts on course material
- **Community Event Participation**: Participate in cybersecurity awareness events, career fairs, or community outreach initiatives

### 9.5 Unapproved Activities

The following activities do NOT count towards CDS:

- Paid employment or freelance work (even if cybersecurity-related)
- Activities primarily benefiting yourself or your employer
- Activities that violate any laws or ethical principles
- Activities not approved in advance by the CDS Coordinator

### 9.6 CDS Planning and Approval

Before starting CDS activities, scholars must:

1. Consult with the ICDFA CDS Coordinator
2. Propose specific CDS activities and describe how they align with CDS objectives
3. Obtain written approval from the CDS Coordinator
4. Identify a supervisor or mentor for each activity

### 9.7 CDS Reporting and Documentation

Scholars must maintain a **CDS Activity Log** that includes:

- Date and time of activity
- Description of activity and learning objectives
- Location and organisation (if applicable)
- Supervisor or mentor name and contact information
- Supervisor sign-off confirming hours and activity completion
- Reflection on impact and learning

Upon completion of all CDS hours, scholars must submit:

- Completed CDS Activity Log with all supervisor sign-offs
- Reflection Report (500-750 words) describing the impact of their CDS work and personal learning

### 9.8 CDS Assessment

CDS activities are assessed based on:

- **Hours Completed**: Minimum 40 hours required
- **Activity Quality**: Meaningfulness and impact of activities
- **Documentation**: Completeness and accuracy of activity logs
- **Reflection**: Depth of reflection on impact and learning

### 9.9 CDS Completion Certificate

Upon successful completion of CDS requirements, scholars receive a **CDS Completion Certificate** recognising their contribution to the community.

### 9.10 Failure to Complete CDS

Failure to complete CDS obligations by the specified deadline may result in:

- Scholarship funds being reclaimed
- Ineligibility for future ICDFA programmes
- Notation on the CCI credential

## 10. What the Scholarship Covers

The ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship covers the following:

- **100% Programme Tuition**: The full USD $150 programme fee
- **Lab Environment Access**: Full access to NDG NETLAB+ and all lab environments
- **Learning Resources**: All course materials, instructional content, and learning resources
- **Partner Platform Access**: Access to Cisco Networking Academy, Palo Alto Networks, Microsoft Azure, AWS Academy, and LPI resources
- **Certification Exam Coupons**: LPI LPIC-1 101 and 102 exam coupons (valued at approximately USD $300)
- **ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI) Credential**: Official certificate and digital badge upon completion
- **Instructor Support**: Access to qualified instructors and teaching assistants
- **Career Services**: Resume review, interview preparation, job board access, and networking opportunities

---

## 11. What the Scholarship Does Not Cover

The ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship does NOT cover the following:

- **Commitment Fee**: USD $10 one-time payment (scholar's responsibility)
- **Computer Equipment**: Laptop or desktop computer (scholar must provide)
- **Internet Service**: Internet connection and related costs (scholar must provide)
- **Additional Industry Certifications**: Certifications beyond LPI LPIC-1 (e.g., CompTIA Security+, CEH)
- **Travel and Accommodation**: Travel to in-person events or accommodation if applicable
- **Meals and Living Expenses**: Daily living expenses during the internship
- **Childcare or Dependent Care**: Costs related to childcare or dependent care
- **Accessibility Accommodations**: Specialised equipment or services for accessibility needs (though ICDFA will work with scholars to identify solutions)

## 12. Scholarship Renewal and Continuation

### 12.1 Completing a Second Track

After successfully completing your first track (Track A or Track B), you may apply for a second scholarship to complete the other track in a future intake.

### 12.2 Eligibility for Second Scholarship

To be eligible for a second scholarship, you must:

- Have successfully completed the first track with a minimum average grade of 75%
- Have completed all CDS obligations from the first scholarship
- Demonstrate continued commitment to cybersecurity
- Meet any updated eligibility criteria

### 12.3 Application for Second Scholarship

To apply for a second scholarship, submit an application for the desired intake following the standard scholarship application process. In your application, highlight your successful completion of the first track and your continued commitment to cybersecurity.

### 12.4 Priority Consideration

Scholars who successfully complete their first track and CDS obligations receive priority consideration for second scholarships, subject to available funding.

## 13. Termination of Scholarship

### 13.1 Grounds for Scholarship Termination

The ICDFA Charity Foundation may terminate a scholarship if:

- The scholar fails any course (grade below 70%)
- The scholar's overall average falls below 75%
- The scholar violates academic integrity policies
- The scholar violates the Code of Conduct or lab discipline policies
- The scholar fails to maintain 90% attendance
- The scholar engages in harassment, discrimination, or bullying
- The scholar fails to complete CDS obligations by the specified deadline
- The scholar withdraws from the programme
- The scholar provides false or misleading information in the scholarship application

### 13.2 Termination Procedure

Upon grounds for termination, the following procedure is followed:

1. The Director of Training notifies the scholar in writing of the grounds for termination
2. The scholar has 5 business days to respond and provide their perspective
3. The Scholarship Committee reviews the case and makes a final decision
4. The scholar is notified of the decision in writing

### 13.3 Consequences of Termination

Upon scholarship termination:

- The scholar's scholarship is immediately revoked
- The scholar's access to lab environments and learning resources is terminated
- The scholar is not eligible for the CCI credential
- Any scholarship funds already disbursed may be reclaimed
- The scholar is ineligible for future ICDFA scholarships
