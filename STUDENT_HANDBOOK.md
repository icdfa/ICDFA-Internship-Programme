## 1. Welcome and Programme Overview

Welcome to the ICDFA Cybersecurity Internship Programme (CIP). This handbook serves as your comprehensive guide to the policies, procedures, and expectations that govern your internship experience. Please read this handbook carefully and retain it for future reference.

The CIP is a rigorous, 6-month, lab-intensive internship designed to develop foundational-to-intermediate cybersecurity skills in either Ethical Hacking or Digital Forensics. The programme combines theoretical knowledge with extensive hands-on laboratory experience delivered through industry-leading platforms including NDG NETLAB+, Cisco Networking Academy, Palo Alto Networks, Microsoft Azure, AWS Academy, and LPI.

Upon successful completion of your selected track, you will be awarded the **ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI)** credential with the relevant specialisation endorsement (Ethical Hacking or Digital Forensics).

## 2. Admission Procedures

### 2.1 Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for admission to the CIP, you must meet the following requirements:

- Be at least 18 years of age
- Possess a high school diploma, GED, or equivalent qualification
- Demonstrate basic computer literacy and command-line familiarity
- Have reliable internet access and a computer meeting minimum technical specifications
- Be able to commit 40-50 hours per week to the programme for 26 consecutive weeks
- Pass the pre-internship readiness assessment (or complete the optional bridge course)

### 2.2 Application Process

The application process consists of the following steps:

**Step 1: Complete the Online Application Form**  
Submit your personal information, educational background, and track preference (Track A: Ethical Hacking or Track B: Digital Forensics).

**Step 2: Submit Supporting Documents**  
Provide copies of your high school diploma or equivalent, government-issued ID, and proof of internet connectivity.

**Step 3: Complete the Pre-Internship Readiness Assessment**  
Take the diagnostic quiz to evaluate your readiness. Scoring "Partially Ready" or above is required for admission.

**Step 4: Attend the Admission Interview**  
Participate in a 30-minute video interview with an ICDFA admissions officer. The interview assesses your motivation, commitment, and suitability for the programme.

**Step 5: Receive Admission Decision**  
Admission decisions are communicated within 5 business days of your interview. Accepted interns receive an admission letter and instructions for enrolment.

### 2.3 Enrolment

Upon receiving your admission letter, you must complete the following to secure your place:

- Sign the Internship Agreement
- Pay the programme fee (self-sponsored track) or confirm scholarship status (scholarship track)
- Complete the IT orientation and NDG NETLAB+ account setup
- Attend the mandatory programme orientation session

## 3. Attendance Policy

### 3.1 Attendance Requirements

Interns are expected to maintain a minimum attendance rate of **90%** across all scheduled sessions, labs, exams, and mandatory events. This includes:

- Scheduled lab sessions
- Live instructor-led sessions (if applicable)
- Chapter exams and midterm exams
- Final comprehensive exams
- Cyber Range assessments
- Mandatory orientation and wrap-up sessions

### 3.2 Unexcused Absences

Unexcused absences exceeding 10% of the total programme hours may result in dismissal from the programme. An absence is considered unexcused if the intern does not notify the Director of Training at least 24 hours in advance.

### 3.3 Excused Absences

Excused absences are permitted for documented reasons including:

- Medical emergencies or illness (with medical certificate)
- Family emergencies (with supporting documentation)
- Religious observances (with advance notice)
- Court-ordered obligations
- Other circumstances approved by the Director of Training

Interns with excused absences must complete missed lab work and assessments within 5 business days of their return.

### 3.4 Tardiness

Interns arriving more than 15 minutes late to a scheduled session will be marked absent for that session. Repeated tardiness (3+ instances) may result in a formal warning.

## 4. Lab Discipline Policy

The lab environments are professional training spaces designed for serious cybersecurity education. All interns must adhere strictly to the following rules at all times:

### 4.1 Prohibited Conduct

- **Unauthorised Access**: Do not access lab environments outside of scheduled sessions or without explicit instructor permission
- **Unauthorised Targets**: Do not use lab tools or techniques against targets outside the designated lab environment
- **Credential Sharing**: Do not share your lab credentials or access with any non-enrolled individual
- **Content Distribution**: Do not record, screenshot, or distribute lab content, assessments, or proprietary tools without authorisation
- **Malicious Activity**: Do not attempt to disrupt, damage, or compromise lab infrastructure or other interns' work
- **Cheating**: Do not submit work that is not your own or collaborate on assessments beyond what is explicitly permitted

### 4.2 Consequences

Violation of any lab discipline rule will result in:

- **First Violation**: Formal written warning and mandatory meeting with Director of Training
- **Second Violation**: Suspension from lab access for up to 2 weeks and academic probation
- **Third Violation**: Immediate removal from the programme with no refund

Serious violations (e.g., malicious activity, credential sharing, content distribution) may result in immediate dismissal regardless of prior warnings.

## 5. No-Retake Policy

The CIP operates under a **strict no-retake policy**. This policy is designed to maintain the integrity and rigour of the programme.

### 5.1 Policy Details

- Interns who fail any course module will **not** be permitted to retake it within the same intake
- Interns who wish to reattempt a failed course must apply for the next available intake of the same track
- Interns who withdraw from the programme may reapply for a future intake, but will start from the beginning
- There is no credit for partially completed courses

### 5.2 Rationale

The no-retake policy ensures that all graduates have completed the full curriculum to the required standard. This maintains the credibility of the ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI) credential and protects the reputation of the ICDFA.

## 6. Academic Integrity Policy

### 6.1 Definition

Academic integrity means submitting work that is entirely your own, properly citing sources, and conducting yourself honestly in all academic matters. The ICDFA takes academic integrity very seriously.

### 6.2 Prohibited Conduct

Academic dishonesty includes:

- **Plagiarism**: Submitting work or ideas from another person without proper attribution
- **Collusion**: Unauthorised collaboration with other interns on assessments
- **Cheating**: Using unauthorised materials, aids, or methods during exams or assessments
- **Fabrication**: Inventing or falsifying data, results, or references
- **Impersonation**: Having another person complete your work or assessments

### 6.3 Consequences

Any instance of academic dishonesty will result in:

- Immediate investigation by the Academic Integrity Committee
- **First Offense**: Failure of the affected assessment or course, and mandatory academic integrity training
- **Second Offense**: Dismissal from the programme with no refund

## 7. Code of Conduct

### 7.1 Professional Conduct

Interns are expected to conduct themselves professionally at all times. This includes:

- **Respectful Communication**: Treat instructors, peers, and support staff with respect and professionalism
- **Timely Submissions**: Submit all assignments, reports, and assessments by the specified deadline
- **Policy Compliance**: Adhere to all ICDFA policies and procedures
- **Confidentiality**: Respect the confidentiality of other interns and ICDFA proprietary information
- **Diversity and Inclusion**: Embrace and respect the diversity of backgrounds, perspectives, and experiences

### 7.2 Unacceptable Conduct

The following conduct is unacceptable and may result in disciplinary action:

- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying of any kind
- Use of offensive language or disruptive behaviour
- Violation of confidentiality or privacy
- Dishonesty or deception
- Violation of intellectual property rights

### 7.3 Disciplinary Process

Violations of the Code of Conduct will be addressed through the following process:

1. **Informal Resolution**: The instructor or staff member addresses the concern directly with the intern
2. **Formal Warning**: If the behaviour continues, a formal written warning is issued
3. **Disciplinary Hearing**: The intern meets with the Director of Training to discuss the violation
4. **Disciplinary Action**: Possible outcomes include probation, suspension, or dismissal

## 8. Assessment and Grading

### 8.1 Assessment Methods

All courses within the CIP are assessed using a combination of the following methods:

**Continuous Assessment (60%)**
- Lab completion and performance grading
- Chapter exams administered after each chapter
- Midterm exams at the midpoint of applicable courses
- Cyber Range performance evaluations

**Final Assessment (40%)**
- Final course exams
- Final comprehensive exams
- Capstone project evaluation (Week 26)
- Professional report submissions (Track B: Advanced Forensic Investigations)

### 8.2 Grading Scale

The following grading scale is used for all courses:

| Grade | Percentage Range | Description |
|-------|------------------|-------------|
| Distinction | 90-100% | Exceptional performance, mastery of content |
| Merit | 80-89% | Strong performance, solid understanding |
| Pass | 70-79% | Satisfactory performance, adequate understanding |
| Fail | Below 70% | Unsatisfactory performance, does not meet requirements |

### 8.3 Progression Requirements

- Interns must achieve a minimum of **70%** in each course to progress to the next course
- Interns must complete all labs with a minimum passing grade to progress
- Interns who fail any course will not be permitted to continue in the programme

### 8.4 Grade Appeals

If you believe your grade is incorrect or unfair, you may submit a grade appeal within 5 business days of receiving your grade. The appeal must include:

- Your name and student ID
- The course code and assessment name
- A detailed explanation of why you believe the grade is incorrect
- Supporting documentation if applicable

Grade appeals are reviewed by the Academic Review Committee and a final decision is communicated within 10 business days.

## 9. Grievance Procedures

### 9.1 Informal Resolution

If you have a concern or complaint, first attempt to resolve it informally by speaking directly with the relevant staff member (instructor, lab coordinator, etc.) within 5 business days of the incident.

### 9.2 Formal Grievance

If the informal resolution does not address your concern, you may file a formal grievance by submitting a written complaint to the Director of Training within 10 business days of the incident. Your complaint should include:

- Your name and student ID
- Date and description of the incident
- Names of individuals involved
- Actions you have already taken to resolve the issue
- Desired resolution

### 9.3 Grievance Investigation

Upon receipt of your formal grievance, the Director of Training will:

1. Acknowledge receipt of your grievance within 2 business days
2. Investigate the complaint impartially and thoroughly
3. Interview relevant parties
4. Compile a written report of findings
5. Communicate the outcome and any corrective actions within 15 business days

### 9.4 Appeals

If you are dissatisfied with the outcome of the grievance investigation, you may appeal to the ICDFA Training Board within 5 business days. The Training Board's decision is final.

## 10. Intern Rights and Responsibilities

### 10.1 Your Rights

As an ICDFA intern, you have the right to:

- Receive high-quality instruction and support from qualified instructors
- Access all necessary lab environments and learning resources
- Receive fair and transparent assessment and grading
- Have your concerns heard and addressed through the grievance procedure
- Privacy and confidentiality of your personal information
- A safe and respectful learning environment free from harassment and discrimination

### 10.2 Your Responsibilities

As an ICDFA intern, you are responsible for:

- Attending all scheduled sessions and completing all required work
- Maintaining academic integrity and honesty in all assessments
- Following all ICDFA policies and procedures
- Respecting the rights and dignity of instructors, peers, and staff
- Protecting the confidentiality of lab content and proprietary tools
- Communicating promptly with instructors about any concerns or challenges
- Seeking help when needed and taking advantage of available support services

## 11. IT Acceptable Use Policy

### 11.1 Scope

This policy applies to all use of ICDFA IT resources, including lab environments, email, learning management systems, and any other technology provided by ICDFA.

### 11.2 Permitted Use

IT resources may be used for:

- Completing assigned coursework and labs
- Accessing learning materials and resources
- Communicating with instructors and peers about coursework
- Professional development related to cybersecurity

### 11.3 Prohibited Use

IT resources may not be used for:

- Accessing or distributing illegal or inappropriate content
- Attempting to gain unauthorised access to systems or data
- Disrupting or interfering with IT services
- Sending spam, viruses, or malicious code
- Violating intellectual property rights
- Harassing or threatening other users
- Commercial or personal business activities unrelated to the programme

### 11.4 Monitoring and Enforcement

ICDFA reserves the right to monitor IT resource usage to ensure compliance with this policy. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including dismissal from the programme.

## 12. Health and Wellbeing Support

### 12.1 Mental Health Support

The ICDFA recognises that the internship programme is intensive and demanding. If you are experiencing stress, anxiety, depression, or other mental health concerns, please reach out to:

- Your instructor or course coordinator
- The ICDFA Wellbeing Support Team (wellbeing@icdfa.org)
- External mental health resources and counselling services (referrals available upon request)

### 12.2 Accessibility and Accommodations

ICDFA is committed to providing an inclusive learning environment for all interns. If you have a disability or require accommodations, please contact the Director of Training as soon as possible to discuss your needs and available support.

### 12.3 Work-Life Balance

While the programme is intensive, it is important to maintain a healthy work-life balance. We encourage you to:

- Take regular breaks during study and lab sessions
- Maintain physical activity and exercise
- Prioritise sleep and nutrition
- Connect with friends and family
- Seek support when needed
