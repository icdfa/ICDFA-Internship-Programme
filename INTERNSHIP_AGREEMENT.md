## 2. Intern Obligations

The intern agrees to:

### 2.1 Attendance and Participation
- Maintain a minimum attendance rate of 90% across all scheduled sessions, labs, exams, and mandatory events
- Arrive on time to all scheduled sessions
- Actively participate in all lab exercises and assessments
- Notify the Director of Training in advance of any anticipated absences

### 2.2 Academic Integrity
- Submit only original work that is entirely the intern's own
- Properly cite and attribute all sources used in assignments and reports
- Not engage in plagiarism, collusion, cheating, or any other form of academic dishonesty
- Report any instances of academic dishonesty to the Director of Training

### 2.3 Lab Discipline
- Adhere strictly to all lab discipline policies and rules
- Use lab credentials and access only for authorised purposes
- Not share lab credentials or access with any non-enrolled individual
- Not record, screenshot, or distribute lab content, assessments, or proprietary tools without authorisation
- Not attempt to disrupt, damage, or compromise lab infrastructure or other interns' work
- Report any security incidents or breaches immediately to the lab coordinator

### 2.4 Timely Submissions
- Submit all assignments, reports, lab work, and assessments by the specified deadlines
- Request extensions in advance if circumstances warrant; extensions are not guaranteed
- Accept the consequences of late submissions, including grade penalties

### 2.5 Professional Conduct
- Conduct themselves professionally and respectfully at all times
- Treat instructors, peers, and support staff with respect and professionalism
- Comply with all ICDFA policies and procedures
- Respect the confidentiality of other interns and ICDFA proprietary information
- Embrace and respect the diversity of backgrounds, perspectives, and experiences

### 2.6 Communication
- Maintain active communication with instructors and support staff
- Promptly respond to emails and messages from ICDFA staff
- Notify the Director of Training immediately of any concerns, challenges, or extenuating circumstances
- Provide accurate and up-to-date contact information

## 4. Confidentiality Clause

### 4.1 Confidential Information
The intern acknowledges that the following materials are confidential and proprietary to ICDFA or its partners:

- Lab content, exercises, and scenarios
- Assessment materials and exam questions
- Proprietary tools and software used in labs
- Course materials and instructional content
- Other materials marked as confidential

### 4.2 Confidentiality Obligations
The intern agrees to:

- Not disclose confidential information to any third party without written authorisation from ICDFA
- Not use confidential information for any purpose other than completing the internship
- Return all confidential materials upon completion or termination of the internship
- Maintain the confidentiality of confidential information even after the internship ends

### 4.3 Duration
Confidentiality obligations continue indefinitely for information that constitutes trade secrets or proprietary methodologies, and for a period of 3 years for other confidential information.

## 5. Acceptable Use of Lab Environments

The intern agrees to use lab environments only for:

- Completing assigned coursework and labs
- Practicing skills taught in the curriculum
- Conducting assessments and exams
- Professional development related to cybersecurity

The intern agrees NOT to use lab environments for:

- Accessing or testing systems outside the designated lab environment
- Attempting to gain unauthorised access to systems or data
- Disrupting or interfering with lab infrastructure
- Conducting attacks or penetration tests without explicit authorisation
- Accessing or distributing illegal or inappropriate content
- Any commercial or personal business activities

## 7. Termination Conditions

### 7.1 Grounds for Dismissal

ICDFA may terminate this agreement and dismiss the intern from the programme for any of the following reasons:

- Failure to maintain 90% attendance rate
- Violation of lab discipline policies
- Academic dishonesty or plagiarism
- Violation of the Code of Conduct
- Harassment, discrimination, or bullying
- Unauthorised access to systems or data
- Disruption of lab infrastructure or other interns' work
- Repeated failure to submit assignments on time
- Failure to pay programme fees (self-sponsored interns)
- Violation of confidentiality or intellectual property rights
- Any other serious violation of ICDFA policies

### 7.2 Dismissal Procedure

In most cases, ICDFA will follow a disciplinary process before dismissal:

1. Formal written warning
2. Disciplinary hearing with the Director of Training
3. Opportunity for the intern to respond
4. Final decision and notification

However, for serious violations (e.g., malicious activity, credential sharing), ICDFA may proceed directly to dismissal without prior warnings.

### 7.3 Consequences of Dismissal

Upon dismissal:

- The intern's access to lab environments and learning resources is immediately terminated
- The intern is not eligible for the CCI credential or any certificates
- The intern forfeits any programme fees paid (no refund)
- For scholarship recipients, the scholarship is terminated and any funds already disbursed may be reclaimed

### 7.4 Voluntary Withdrawal

If the intern wishes to withdraw from the programme, they must submit a written request to the Director of Training. Upon withdrawal:

- The intern's access to lab environments and learning resources is terminated
- The intern is not eligible for the CCI credential
- For self-sponsored interns, programme fees are non-refundable
- For scholarship recipients, the scholarship is terminated

## 8. Scholarship-Specific Clauses (ICDFA Charity Foundation)

*This section applies only to interns receiving an ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship.*

### 8.1 Scholarship Coverage

The ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship covers:

- 100% of programme tuition fees
- Full access to all lab environments and learning resources
- Certification exam coupons (LPI LPIC-1 101 and 102)
- Access to partner platforms (NDG NETLAB+, Cisco, Palo Alto Networks, Microsoft Azure, AWS)

### 8.2 Commitment Fee

The intern agrees to pay a one-time commitment fee of **USD $10** upon enrolment. This fee demonstrates the intern's commitment to completing the programme and is non-refundable.

### 8.3 Community Development Service (CDS)

The intern agrees to complete a minimum of **40 hours** of Community Development Service (CDS) during or immediately after the internship. Approved CDS activities include:

- Cybersecurity awareness workshops for schools or community organisations
- IT mentoring for underprivileged youth
- Community digital literacy training
- Open-source contributions to cybersecurity projects
- Technical support for local NGOs
- Cybersecurity blog writing or content creation
- Peer tutoring for future CIP cohorts

The intern must:

- Plan CDS activities in consultation with the ICDFA CDS Coordinator
- Complete CDS activities by the end of the internship or within 3 months after completion
- Maintain a CDS activity log with supervisor sign-offs
- Submit a reflection report upon completion

Failure to complete CDS obligations may result in forfeiture of the scholarship and reclamation of scholarship funds.

### 8.4 Academic Performance

Scholarship recipients are expected to maintain strong academic performance. Scholarship may be revoked if:

- The intern fails any course
- The intern's overall grade falls below 75%
- The intern violates academic integrity policies
