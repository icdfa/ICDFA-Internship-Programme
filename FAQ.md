## 1. Admission and Application

**Q1.1: What are the basic eligibility requirements to apply for the CIP?**

A: To be eligible for the CIP, you must be at least 18 years old, possess a high school diploma or equivalent, demonstrate basic computer literacy and command-line familiarity, have reliable internet access, and be able to commit 40-50 hours per week for 26 consecutive weeks.

**Q1.2: How long does the application process take?**

A: The complete application process typically takes 2-3 weeks from submission to final admission decision. This includes document review, readiness assessment, admission interview, and decision communication.

**Q1.3: What documents do I need to submit with my application?**

A: You will need to submit: (1) completed online application form, (2) copy of high school diploma or equivalent, (3) government-issued ID, (4) proof of internet connectivity, and (5) results from the pre-internship readiness assessment.

**Q1.4: Is the pre-internship readiness assessment mandatory?**

A: Yes, the readiness assessment is mandatory. However, if you score "Partially Ready," you may be offered the option to complete an optional bridge course before starting the main programme.

**Q1.5: What happens during the admission interview?**

A: The admission interview is a 30-minute video conversation with an ICDFA admissions officer. They will assess your motivation, commitment, suitability for the programme, and answer any questions you have. The interview is conversational and not adversarial.

**Q1.6: How will I know if I've been accepted?**

A: Admission decisions are communicated within 5 business days of your interview via email. Accepted interns receive an admission letter with instructions for enrolment.

**Q1.7: Can I defer my admission to a later intake?**

A: Yes, you may request to defer your admission to the next available intake. Deferrals must be requested in writing to the Director of Training within 10 days of receiving your admission letter.

**Q1.8: What if I don't meet all the eligibility requirements?**

A: If you fall short in any area, you may be offered conditional admission with the requirement to complete preparatory coursework or a bridge course before starting the main programme.

## 2. Programme Details and Schedule

**Q2.1: How long is the CIP programme?**

A: The CIP is a 26-week (6-month) intensive programme. Both Track A (Ethical Hacking) and Track B (Digital Forensics) follow the same 26-week timeline.

**Q2.2: What is the weekly time commitment?**

A: Interns are expected to commit 40-50 hours per week to the programme. This includes scheduled lab sessions, live instruction, independent study, and assessments.

**Q2.3: What are the programme hours?**

A: Programme hours are typically Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM (local time), with breaks. Specific schedules will be provided upon enrolment.

**Q2.4: Can I study part-time or at my own pace?**

A: No, the CIP is a full-time, cohort-based programme. All interns must follow the same 26-week schedule and complete courses in the prescribed sequence.

**Q2.5: What is the difference between Track A and Track B?**

A: Track A (Ethical Hacking) focuses on offensive security skills including reconnaissance, scanning, exploitation, and social engineering. Track B (Digital Forensics) focuses on digital evidence acquisition, forensic analysis, and incident response. Both tracks are 26 weeks but cover different content.

**Q2.6: Can I switch tracks after starting the programme?**

A: No, you cannot switch tracks mid-programme. However, after completing your first track, you may enrol in the second track in a future intake.

**Q2.7: What happens during Week 26?**

A: Week 26 is dedicated to final assessments, capstone reviews, and programme wrap-up. This includes final exams, capstone project presentations, and certification ceremonies.

**Q2.8: Are there any scheduled breaks during the 26 weeks?**

A: The programme runs continuously for 26 weeks with minimal breaks. Specific break dates will be communicated upon enrolment and will align with major public holidays.

## 3. Technical Requirements

**Q3.1: What are the minimum computer specifications required?**

A: Minimum specifications are: Processor: Intel i5 or equivalent (6th Gen or newer), RAM: 8GB (16GB recommended), Storage: 256GB SSD, Internet: 10 Mbps minimum (25 Mbps recommended), Operating System: Windows 10/11, macOS 10.14+, or Linux (Ubuntu 18.04+).

**Q3.2: Do I need to purchase any software?**

A: No, all software and tools used in the programme are provided through the NDG NETLAB+ platform or are open-source. There are no software purchase requirements.

**Q3.3: What internet speed do I need?**

A: A minimum of 10 Mbps download speed is required. However, 25 Mbps or higher is recommended for optimal performance, especially during live lab sessions.

**Q3.4: Can I use a laptop or tablet instead of a desktop computer?**

A: Yes, you can use a laptop. However, tablets are not recommended as they lack the necessary processing power and keyboard functionality for intensive lab work.

**Q3.5: What if my internet connection is unstable?**

A: If you experience frequent internet outages, please notify the Director of Training immediately. You may be offered accommodations such as extended deadlines for assessments or access to a backup internet location.

**Q3.6: Do I need to install any software before the programme starts?**

A: Yes, you will need to install VirtualBox (free) and set up your NDG NETLAB+ account. Detailed setup instructions will be provided during the orientation session.

**Q3.7: What if I have technical issues during the programme?**

A: Contact the Technical Support team immediately at support@icdfa.org or call +1 (555) 123-4570. They will troubleshoot and resolve issues as quickly as possible.

**Q3.8: Is there a backup plan if the NDG NETLAB+ platform goes down?**

A: Yes, ICDFA maintains backup lab environments and alternative access methods. In the event of a platform outage, interns will be notified and provided with alternative access instructions.

## 4. Cost and Funding

**Q4.1: What is the cost of the CIP programme?**

A: The self-sponsored track costs USD $150. The ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship covers 100% of tuition with a one-time commitment fee of USD $10.

**Q4.2: What does the programme fee include?**

A: The programme fee includes: full access to all lab environments, all course materials and instructional content, access to partner platforms (NDG NETLAB+, Cisco, Palo Alto Networks, Microsoft Azure, AWS), certification exam coupons (LPI LPIC-1 101 and 102), and the ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI) credential upon completion.

**Q4.3: Are there any additional fees or hidden costs?**

A: No, there are no additional fees. The programme fee is all-inclusive. However, if you choose to pursue additional industry certifications beyond what is included, those costs are your responsibility.

**Q4.4: What payment methods are accepted?**

A: Payment methods include: credit card (Visa, Mastercard, American Express), bank transfer, and PayPal. Payment must be made upon enrolment.

**Q4.5: Is there a refund policy?**

A: No, programme fees are non-refundable. However, if you withdraw before the programme starts, you may request a refund within 14 days of payment.

**Q4.6: How do I apply for the ICDFA Charity Foundation Scholarship?**

A: During the application process, you will indicate your interest in the scholarship. If you are selected for admission, you will be notified of your scholarship status. Scholarship recipients must meet specific eligibility criteria including demonstrated financial need.

**Q4.7: How many scholarship slots are available?**

A: Up to 50 scholarship slots are available per intake, depending on funding availability.

**Q4.8: What is the commitment fee for scholarship recipients?**

A: Scholarship recipients pay a one-time commitment fee of USD $10 upon enrolment. This fee demonstrates the intern's commitment to completing the programme and is non-refundable.

## 5. Track Selection

**Q5.1: How do I choose between Track A and Track B?**

A: Consider your career interests and strengths. Track A (Ethical Hacking) is ideal if you want to work in offensive security roles such as penetration tester, security engineer, or SOC analyst. Track B (Digital Forensics) is ideal if you want to work in forensic analysis, incident response, or eDiscovery roles.

**Q5.2: Can I do both tracks simultaneously?**

A: No, you must select one track and complete it fully. After completing your first track, you may enrol in the second track in a future intake.

**Q5.3: What is the difference in workload between the two tracks?**

A: Both tracks have similar workloads (40-50 hours per week). Track A has 10 courses and 148 labs. Track B has 7 courses and 122 labs. The difference in lab count is due to the nature of the content, not the difficulty level.

**Q5.4: Which track is more difficult?**

A: Both tracks are equally rigorous and challenging. The difficulty depends on your background and interests. If you have strong Linux and networking skills, Track A may feel more natural. If you have strong investigative and analytical skills, Track B may suit you better.

**Q5.5: Can I change my track after I've been admitted?**

A: You may request a track change before enrolment. However, once the programme starts, track changes are not permitted.

**Q5.6: What careers can I pursue after Track A?**

A: Track A graduates can pursue roles such as: Penetration Tester, Ethical Hacker, Security Engineer, SOC Analyst, ICS Security Specialist, Cloud Security Engineer, and Offensive Security Consultant.

**Q5.7: What careers can I pursue after Track B?**

A: Track B graduates can pursue roles such as: Digital Forensic Examiner, Incident Response Analyst, Malware Analyst, eDiscovery Specialist, Computer Forensic Analyst, and Forensic Consultant.

**Q5.8: Do I need prior experience to succeed in either track?**

A: No, both tracks are designed for beginners. However, basic computer literacy and familiarity with command-line interfaces are helpful.

## 6. Labs and Hands-On Training

**Q6.1: How many labs are in each track?**

A: Track A includes 148 labs across 10 courses. Track B includes 122 labs across 7 courses.

**Q6.2: What is the format of the labs?**

A: Labs are hands-on, practical exercises conducted in virtual lab environments provided by NDG NETLAB+. Each lab includes step-by-step instructions, learning objectives, and assessment criteria.

**Q6.3: How much time should I spend on each lab?**

A: Lab duration varies from 1-3 hours depending on complexity. The weekly schedule indicates the expected time allocation for each lab.

**Q6.4: Can I access labs outside of scheduled times?**

A: Yes, you can access labs 24/7 through the NDG NETLAB+ platform. However, live instructor support is available only during scheduled lab sessions.

**Q6.5: What if I don't complete a lab on time?**

A: Late lab submissions will incur a grade penalty. You must complete labs by the specified deadline to progress to the next course.

**Q6.6: What is the Cyber Range?**

A: The Cyber Range is an advanced, scenario-based lab environment where interns apply skills learned across multiple courses to solve complex, realistic cybersecurity challenges. Cyber Range assessments occur at the end of applicable courses.

**Q6.7: How are labs graded?**

A: Labs are graded on a pass/fail basis or on a percentage scale (0-100%). Grading criteria include: completion of all required tasks, correct execution of commands/procedures, and achievement of learning objectives.

**Q6.8: Can I retake a lab if I fail?**

A: No, under the no-retake policy, if you fail a lab, you cannot retake it. However, you may request feedback from the instructor to understand where you went wrong.

## 7. Assessment and Grading

**Q7.1: How are interns assessed in the CIP?**

A: Assessment is continuous and includes: lab completion and grading (60%), chapter exams, midterm exams, final exams, and capstone project evaluation (40%).

**Q7.2: What is the grading scale?**

A: Distinction (90-100%), Merit (80-89%), Pass (70-79%), Fail (below 70%). A minimum of 70% is required to pass each course.

**Q7.3: What is the passing grade?**

A: The minimum passing grade is 70%. Interns must achieve at least 70% in each course to progress to the next course.

**Q7.4: How often are grades communicated?**

A: Grades are communicated after each chapter exam, midterm exam, and final exam. Lab grades are communicated upon completion. Final course grades are communicated at the end of each course.

**Q7.5: Can I appeal a grade?**

A: Yes, you may submit a grade appeal within 5 business days of receiving your grade. The appeal must include a detailed explanation of why you believe the grade is incorrect, supported by evidence.

**Q7.6: What happens if I fail a course?**

A: Under the no-retake policy, if you fail a course, you cannot continue in the programme. You may reapply for the next intake to retake the course.

**Q7.7: Is there a cumulative GPA requirement?**

A: No, there is no cumulative GPA requirement. However, you must achieve a minimum of 70% in each individual course.

**Q7.8: How is the final grade calculated?**

A: Final grade = (Continuous Assessment 60%) + (Final Assessment 40%). Continuous Assessment includes lab grades and chapter exams. Final Assessment includes final exams and capstone project.

## 8. Certifications and Credentials

**Q8.1: What credential do I receive upon completion?**

A: You receive the ICDFA Certified Cybersecurity Intern (CCI) credential with a specialisation endorsement (Ethical Hacking or Digital Forensics).

**Q8.2: Is the CCI credential recognised internationally?**

A: The CCI credential is recognised within the cybersecurity industry and by ICDFA partner organisations. It demonstrates foundational-to-intermediate cybersecurity competency in your selected specialisation.

**Q8.3: What industry certifications can I pursue after the CIP?**

A: Upon completion, you are eligible for: LPI LPIC-1 (Linux Professional Institute), CompTIA Security+, and other vendor certifications depending on your track.

**Q8.4: Are industry certification exam coupons included?**

A: Yes, LPI LPIC-1 101 and 102 exam coupons are included in the programme fee for all interns.

**Q8.5: How do I use the exam coupons?**

A: Exam coupons will be provided upon completion of the relevant courses. Instructions for registering and scheduling exams will be included with the coupons.

**Q8.6: Can I pursue the CCI credential and industry certifications simultaneously?**

A: Yes, you can work towards both. However, industry certifications require additional study and exam preparation beyond the programme curriculum.

**Q8.7: What is the validity period of the CCI credential?**

A: The CCI credential is valid indefinitely. However, ICDFA may require periodic renewal or continuing education to maintain credential status (details TBD).

**Q8.8: How do I display the CCI credential?**

A: You will receive a digital badge and certificate upon completion. You can display the badge on your LinkedIn profile, resume, and professional websites.

## 9. Policies and Conduct

**Q9.1: What is the attendance requirement?**

A: Interns must maintain a minimum attendance rate of 90% across all scheduled sessions, labs, exams, and mandatory events.

**Q9.2: What happens if I miss a class or lab?**

A: Absences are tracked and counted against your 90% attendance requirement. Unexcused absences exceeding 10% of total programme hours may result in dismissal.

**Q9.3: What is the no-retake policy?**

A: Under the no-retake policy, if you fail any course or lab, you cannot retake it within the same intake. You must reapply for a future intake to retry.

**Q9.4: What is academic integrity?**

A: Academic integrity means submitting work that is entirely your own, properly citing sources, and conducting yourself honestly in all academic matters. Violations include plagiarism, collusion, cheating, and fabrication.

**Q9.5: What are the consequences of academic dishonesty?**

A: First offense: failure of the affected assessment/course and mandatory academic integrity training. Second offense: dismissal from the programme with no refund.

**Q9.6: What is the lab discipline policy?**

A: The lab discipline policy prohibits unauthorised access, credential sharing, content distribution, malicious activity, and cheating. Violations result in formal warnings, suspension, or dismissal.

**Q9.7: What is the Code of Conduct?**

A: The Code of Conduct requires professional, respectful behaviour; timely submissions; policy compliance; confidentiality; and diversity and inclusion. Violations include harassment, discrimination, bullying, and dishonesty.

**Q9.8: What happens if I violate the Code of Conduct?**

A: Violations are addressed through a disciplinary process: informal resolution, formal warning, disciplinary hearing, and possible disciplinary action (probation, suspension, or dismissal).

## 11. Technical Issues and Troubleshooting

**Q11.1: What should I do if I can't access the NDG NETLAB+ platform?**

A: First, check your internet connection. If the connection is stable, try clearing your browser cache and restarting your browser. If the issue persists, contact Technical Support at support@icdfa.org or +1 (555) 123-4570.

**Q11.2: What if a lab environment crashes or becomes unresponsive?**

A: Stop the lab, wait 5 minutes, and try again. If the issue persists, contact the lab coordinator immediately. They will investigate and provide alternative access if necessary.

**Q11.3: How do I reset my lab credentials if I forget my password?**

A: Use the "Forgot Password" link on the NDG NETLAB+ login page. Follow the instructions to reset your password. If you don't receive a reset email, contact Technical Support.

**Q11.4: Can I use a VPN to access the lab environments?**

A: VPNs are generally not recommended as they may slow down lab performance. However, if you need to use a VPN for security reasons, contact Technical Support to ensure compatibility.

**Q11.5: What if my computer crashes during a lab?**

A: If your computer crashes, restart it and log back into the NDG NETLAB+ platform. Your lab progress is typically saved automatically. If you lose work, contact the lab coordinator for assistance.

**Q11.6: How do I download lab materials or resources?**

A: Lab materials are typically available within the NDG NETLAB+ platform. You can download resources as needed. If you have difficulty accessing specific materials, contact Technical Support.

**Q11.7: What if I experience lag or slow performance during labs?**

A: Lag can be caused by internet connection issues, computer performance, or platform overload. Try: (1) restarting your computer, (2) closing unnecessary applications, (3) checking your internet speed, (4) trying a different browser. If issues persist, contact Technical Support.

**Q11.8: Is there a help desk or support chat available during labs?**

A: Yes, live technical support is available during scheduled lab sessions. You can access support through the NDG NETLAB+ platform or by calling +1 (555) 123-4570.
