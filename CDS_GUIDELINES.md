# ICDFA Internship Programme -- Community Development Service (CDS) Guidelines

**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** April 2026

---

## Overview

The Community Development Service (CDS) is a mandatory component of the ICDFA Cybersecurity Internship Programme. CDS requires interns to contribute to their communities through cybersecurity education, awareness, or support activities. This policy applies to all interns in both Track A (Ethical Hacking) and Track B (Digital Forensics).

---

## CDS Requirements

### Mandatory Hours

All interns must complete a minimum of **40 hours of Community Development Service** during their 26-week internship.

- **Minimum:** 40 hours
- **Recommended:** 50-60 hours
- **Maximum:** No upper limit (encouraged to exceed minimum)

### Timing

CDS hours should be distributed throughout the internship:

- **Weeks 1-8:** 0-10 hours (foundation phase, focus on coursework)
- **Weeks 9-18:** 10-20 hours (mid-programme, increase CDS engagement)
- **Weeks 19-26:** 10-20 hours (final phase, capstone CDS projects)

---

## Approved CDS Activities

### Category 1: Cybersecurity Education & Awareness

Interns may earn CDS hours for:

**1.1 Community Workshops & Training**
- Conduct free cybersecurity awareness workshops for schools, non-profits, or community centers
- Deliver presentations on topics such as password security, phishing awareness, or safe browsing
- **Hours:** 2 hours per workshop (1 hour prep + 1 hour delivery)

**1.2 Mentoring & Tutoring**
- Mentor younger students or career-changers interested in cybersecurity
- Provide one-on-one tutoring on Linux, networking, or security concepts
- **Hours:** 1 hour per mentoring session

**1.3 Cybersecurity Bootcamp Assistance**
- Assist ICDFA instructors in delivering bootcamps or training programmes
- Help with lab setup, student support, or assessment grading
- **Hours:** 1 hour per hour of bootcamp assistance

**1.4 Online Content Creation**
- Create educational blog posts, videos, or tutorials on cybersecurity topics
- Contribute to cybersecurity knowledge bases or wikis
- **Hours:** 2-3 hours per piece of content (depending on complexity)

### Category 2: Cybersecurity Support & Advocacy

Interns may earn CDS hours for:

**2.1 Cybersecurity Awareness Campaigns**
- Develop and execute cybersecurity awareness campaigns in your community
- Create posters, infographics, or social media content on security topics
- **Hours:** 3-5 hours per campaign

**2.2 Cybersecurity Policy Advocacy**
- Advocate for better cybersecurity practices in local organizations
- Help non-profits develop cybersecurity policies or incident response plans
- **Hours:** 2-4 hours per policy development project

**2.3 Cybersecurity Incident Support**
- Volunteer to assist organizations affected by cybersecurity incidents
- Provide pro-bono incident response or forensics support (under instructor supervision)
- **Hours:** 2-4 hours per incident support project

**2.4 Cybersecurity Community Events**
- Organize or participate in cybersecurity conferences, meetups, or networking events
- Volunteer at cybersecurity awareness events
- **Hours:** 1-2 hours per event

### Category 3: Professional Development & Contribution

Interns may earn CDS hours for:

**3.1 Open-Source Security Projects**
- Contribute to open-source security tools or projects
- Report bugs, create documentation, or develop new features
- **Hours:** 1-2 hours per contribution

**3.2 Cybersecurity Research & Publication**
- Conduct original cybersecurity research
- Publish findings in blogs, whitepapers, or academic journals
- **Hours:** 5-10 hours per publication

**3.3 Cybersecurity Certification Study Groups**
- Lead study groups for industry certifications (CEH, Security+, GCIH, etc.)
- Help peers prepare for certification exams
- **Hours:** 1 hour per study group session

**3.4 Cybersecurity Mentorship from Professionals**
- Organize mentorship sessions with industry professionals
- Facilitate networking between interns and cybersecurity practitioners
- **Hours:** 2-3 hours per mentorship session organized

## CDS Approval Process

### Step 1: Proposal Submission

Before beginning a CDS activity, interns must submit a **CDS Activity Proposal** to their instructor or the CDS Coordinator. The proposal should include:

- **Activity Title:** Clear, descriptive title
- **Activity Description:** What will be done and why
- **Community Benefit:** How will this benefit the community?
- **Duration:** Estimated hours required
- **Timeline:** When will this be completed?
- **Deliverables:** What will be produced or achieved?

### Step 2: Approval

The CDS Coordinator will review the proposal and approve or request modifications within 5 business days.

**Approval Criteria:**
- Activity aligns with cybersecurity education, awareness, or support goals
- Activity is feasible within the proposed timeline
- Activity provides genuine community benefit
- Activity does not conflict with internship coursework

### Step 3: Execution

Once approved, interns may begin the CDS activity. Interns should:

- Keep detailed records of hours spent
- Document deliverables and outcomes
- Collect feedback from community members (if applicable)
- Take photos or screenshots as evidence

### Step 4: Reporting & Verification

Upon completion, interns must submit a **CDS Activity Report** including:

- **Activity Summary:** What was accomplished?
- **Hours Logged:** Total hours spent (with breakdown by date)
- **Deliverables:** What was produced?
- **Community Impact:** How did this benefit the community?
- **Evidence:** Photos, testimonials, or documentation
- **Instructor/Supervisor Sign-Off:** Verification from activity supervisor

## CDS Verification & Compliance

### Verification Requirements

All CDS hours must be verified by:

- **Activity Supervisor:** Person who directly supervised the activity
- **Instructor Sign-Off:** ICDFA instructor confirmation
- **Documentation:** Photos, testimonials, or deliverables

### Compliance Monitoring

- CDS Coordinator will monitor progress at weeks 8, 18, and 26
- Interns falling behind on CDS hours will receive a warning and support plan
- Failure to complete 40 CDS hours will result in programme extension or dismissal

### Consequences of Non-Compliance

- **Below 40 hours at Week 26:** Internship not completed; must complete remaining hours before graduation
- **Falsified CDS Records:** Immediate dismissal from programme
- **Unethical CDS Activities:** Immediate dismissal from programme

## CDS Recognition & Rewards

### CDS Certificates

Interns who complete 40+ CDS hours will receive a **CDS Certificate of Recognition** acknowledging their community contribution.

### CDS Honors

- **CDS Excellence Award:** Interns completing 60+ hours with exceptional impact
- **CDS Leadership Award:** Interns who organize or lead major CDS initiatives
- **CDS Recognition:** Public acknowledgment on ICDFA website and social media

### Alumni CDS Opportunities

Graduates are encouraged to continue CDS activities as alumni, including:

- Mentoring future interns

- Serving on ICDFA advisory boards

## CDS Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I count paid work as CDS?**  
A: No. CDS must be volunteer work. Paid employment does not count toward CDS hours.

**Q: Can I count coursework or labs as CDS?**  
A: No. CDS must be separate from required internship coursework.

**Q: Can I do CDS activities remotely?**  
A: Yes. Virtual workshops, online mentoring, and remote contributions are all acceptable.

**Q: What if I exceed 40 CDS hours?**  
A: Excellent! Exceeding the minimum demonstrates exceptional commitment. Extra hours may be recognized with CDS Honors.

**Q: Can I change my CDS activity if approved?**  
A: Yes. Submit a modification request to the CDS Coordinator. Minor changes are typically approved quickly.

**Q: What if I cannot complete 40 hours due to circumstances?**  
A: Contact the CDS Coordinator immediately. Accommodations may be available for documented hardships.
